2. Executive signal-quality summary

Overall trust levelLow to moderate. The portfolio view is usable for cleanup planning and governance follow-up, but not reliable enough for executive tradeoff decisions, capacity decisions, or prioritization until the most material defects are remediated.
Primary reporting riskActive work is likely overstated because some records appear active while dates are stale, ownership is incomplete, sponsors are missing, or readiness is not clear.
Highest-risk patternRecords with missing sponsor, stale last-update date, and active or at-risk status. These records create false confidence because they can appear governed while lacking accountable decision ownership.
Immediate decision asksConfirm sponsors for orphaned work, decide whether duplicate demand should be merged or linked, determine whether stale active items remain active, and define minimum KPI hygiene before records are sent to scoring.
Recommended postureDo not use this portfolio view as a decision baseline until Wave 1 cleanup is complete. Use it as a signal-quality backlog and governance follow-up list.

7. Executive summary output

The portfolio source is not yet reliable enough to support executive prioritization or capacity tradeoff decisions. The largest concern is not one bad field; it is the combination of stale active statuses, missing sponsorship, duplicate demand candidates, and weak KPI definitions. These defects can make the portfolio look more controlled than it is. The recommended next step is a focused cleanup cycle: confirm decision ownership, normalize status values, resolve duplicate candidates, classify readiness, and define minimum KPI hygiene before sending records into intake, scoring, or executive review.
